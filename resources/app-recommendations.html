Pitch Tools

Voice Tools (app):

  • Available on Apple App Store and Google Play Store
  • Disable the "Show Gender Markers" option in Settings. The "Pitch" tab shows a flowing chart of fundamental frequency. The "Tone" tab reads real-time fundamental frequency and has tones to select from for the student to play a starting pitch. There is also a spectrogram in the "More" tab.
  • Note, there is a voice pitch analyzer in the "Analyse" tab. Generally, we discourage use of voice pitch analyzers. Talk to your instructor about this if you have further questions.

Spectrogram from Listening to Waves (website)

  • Free to use, click the microphone icon to get started. Works best on desktop, not so much on mobile devices.

Recording Tools

  • Vocaroo.com (website): Great for recording, and sharing recordings. You can download their recordings or save/share via link. Works in-browser on desktop and mobile.
  • On Apple mobile devices, Voice Memos
  • On Android mobile devices, Voice Recorder

Reading Resources

Random sentence resources:

  • http://practice.herostrat.us/ - Three Harvard sentences at a time. Created by a trans person doing voice training, for trans people doing voice training!
  • https://perchance.org/sentence - Select the number of sentences you want at a time.

Longer reading resources:

  • Rock Paper Shotgun: Fun, creatively written video game reviews.
  • Random Wikipedia Pages: Re-click, copy and paste "https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special:Random" in the search bar, or select "Random article" from the main menu on Wikipedia for seemingly endless articles!
  • Tongue twisters: Organized here by starting letter.
  • Words and sentences by letter and structure: Good for targeting specific letters, and ramping up new reading material.

Other

  • Random emotion generator: Great for work during embodiment/modification, reading or improvising with different emotions. Can generate one or multiple emotions at a time.

Images to have students describe:
